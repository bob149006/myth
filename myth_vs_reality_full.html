Myth vs. Reality

Myth
You don't need gutter protection if you don’t have trees.
Leaves and debris often clog gutters, but birds, rodents, and insects can also contribute by nesting or leaving droppings. Wind and storms may bring additional dirt and debris. Installing gutter guards helps prevent these clogs and reduces the need for frequent cleaning, even in areas without trees.
Myth
Gutter guards eliminate the need for gutter maintenance.
Gutter guards reduce debris but don’t eliminate maintenance; consider using The 360 Degree Utility Cleaning Brush, explicitly crafted to clean any gutter guard effortlessly.
Myth
Gutter guards are only effective in certain climates.
Some homeowners mistakenly think gutter guards are only helpful in areas with heavy rainfall or many trees, but they benefit any climate. Even in drier regions, gutter guards prevent dust, dirt, and debris accumulation in gutters, reducing the need for frequent cleaning.
Myth
Gutter guards are unsightly and decrease curb appeal.
Homeowners concerned about the look of gutter guards can find modern, discreet options that blend with their roofline and are virtually invisible from the ground. These designs protect effectively and enhance or maintain the home's curb appeal.
Myth
Gutter guards are expensive and not worth the investment.
Installing gutter guards initially costs more but saves in the long run by preventing clogs, reducing cleanings, and extending gutter lifespan. They also improve gutter efficiency, ensuring rainwater flows freely and reducing home water damage risks.
Myth
One product can do it all regardless of my home's environment.
Certain settings and climates are best served by specific solutions optimized for the conditions. Considering debris loads, tree species, common pests, typical rainfall, filtration, and fire prevention will help you select the right coverage at the correct cost.
Myth
Gutter guards do not last and can collapse under the weight of heavier debris.
Gutter guards made of cheap materials can degrade over time and collapse under average to heavier debris loads. Choose high-quality, weather resistant gutter guards for long-term protection and performance.
